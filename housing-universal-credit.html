Housing and Universal Credit

You could get money to help pay your housing costs. Your eligibility and how much you get depends on your age and circumstances.

The payment can cover:

  • rent
  • mortgage interest
  • some service charges
  • interest on a loan secured against your home

If you're currently on Housing Benefit

Universal Credit is replacing Housing Benefit in stages across Great Britain and Northern Ireland.

If you’re currently getting Housing Benefit, you don’t need to do anything until you hear from Department for Work and Pensions or there’s a change in your circumstances, for example you move house.

Paying your rent

If you’re on Universal Credit you’ll get one monthly payment that can include housing costs.

You need to pay rent directly to your landlord. Speak to your landlord to agree the best way to pay them, for example you might set up a standing order to pay your rent and other bills like gas and electricity.

You need to know how much your monthly rent is when you apply. You’ll also need a contract or a written agreement with your landlord.

You can also get help with some service charges. Landlords can tell you which charges are supported by Universal Credit and the amount you can get support for.

If you get into difficulty paying your rent

If you’re at least 2 months in arrears with your rent you or your landlord can apply for an Alternative Payment Arrangement (APA). This means your rent can be paid directly to your landlord or you get a payment every 2 weeks instead of monthly.

Paying your mortgage

If you or your partner own the home you live in, your Universal Credit payment may include Support for Mortgage Interest (SMI). The amount you receive is based on a set rate of interest on your outstanding mortgage. It will be paid direct to your mortgage lender.

If your house is leasehold you can also get help with some service charges.

Evidence you need to provide

When you apply for Universal Credit you need to provide evidence of your rent or mortgage payments, for example:

  • current tenancy agreement
  • current rent statement
  • current rent book
  • signed letter from your landlord
  • mortgage agreement
  • current mortgage statement
  • any loan agreement/s secured on property
  • bank statement showing mortgage payments

Department for Work and Pensions (DWP) will check your evidence before paying housing costs.

If you're between 18 and 21

If you’re between 18 to 21, you won’t usually get help with housing costs if you make a new claim for Universal Credit, live in a full service area and you:

  • aren’t working
  • are working but your earnings are below the minimum in-work threshold (for 18 to 20 year olds who are not apprentices this is £89.60 a week)

There are some exceptions when you can get help with housing costs.

Council Tax

You may be able to get help from your local authority with your Council Tax.

The help you will get depends on where you live, your circumstances, your household income - including savings and pensions, if any children or adults live with you and your local council’s own Council Tax reduction scheme.

Sheltered and supported housing

You live in sheltered or supported housing, for example you stay in a hostel or a women’s refuge, you might not get the housing element of Universal Credit. You’ll need to claim Housing Benefit instead.
